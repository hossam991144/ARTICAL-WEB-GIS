Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and their role in developing smart infrastructure

➤ Geographic Information Systems (GIS) are among the most important modern technologies that have revolutionized the way spatial data is managed and analyzed. It is no longer just about maps; it has become an integrated decision-making system that relies on geographic location as a fundamental element of analysis.

  • What is GIS?
    It is a system that integrates:
    1. Spatial Data → represents location (point – line – polygon).
    2. Attribute Data → represents information related to the site.
    3. Advanced analytical tools.
    4. Strong databases.

  • The importance of GIS in infrastructure projects
    In road, water, drainage, electricity, and telecommunications projects, GIS plays a pivotal role in:
    1. Utility Network Management
      Through systems such as ArcGIS Utility Network
    2. Supporting the decision-maker
      Using tools such as ArcGIS Pro
    3. Integration with engineering systems
      There is now direct integration between GIS and design software such as AutoCAD and Civil 3D

  • GIS and Smart Cities
    As part of the digital transformation, smart cities rely on GIS in:
    1. Traffic Department
    2. Service distribution
    3. Monitoring energy consumption
    4. Urban planning
    5. Disaster Management

Furthermore, the important role of Geographic Information Systems (GIS) is to provide the best services to everyone and to help in the best ways to access services through Google Earth or Google Maps.
